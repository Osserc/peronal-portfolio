A grab bag of games

As part of learning the Ruby language, I tested my understanding by building a variety of games.

It has a few different pastimes, like Hangman, Mastermind and Connect 4, of ever increasing complexity.

In the process of building them, I faced challenges like accounting for win conditions, preventing invalid moves and serializing data.

I also had a taste of implementing algorithms when it came to make the computer part of Mastermind. Making it guess the code correctly was a good challenge.

Do check them out.


Data structures

Working with data can be rather hard without a proper structure behind them.

These projects were all about organizing data for easier navigation.

The first two concerned the making of an infrastructure for linked lists and binary search trees.

The last one, Knight's Travails, required me to apply what I learned to solve the problem of getting a Knight from square A to square B in the least moves possible.

This meant that I also had to come up with a way to represent a chess board and to validate the moves of the knight, which came in handy when building chess proper.
